This page allows you to checkout the underwater robots in action! If you see a moving ship with animated icons moving around, it means that right now, we are out at sea using robots to gather underwater images and oceanographic data. This data is used for many purposes including habitat mapping, change detection, monitoring biodiversity and change detection. In this collaborative process, the robots gather imagery and then the scientists identify and label the content in the pictures. This helps scientists understand what is out there and how it's changing through time.

These underwater robots are producing massive amounts imagery: tens of thousands of pictures can be collected by a single robot in a day. It would be impossible for scientists to look at every single image and label every object within them. We use the relatively few labels we get from marine scientist to train computer algorithms to classify the data. The more labels we get, the better the classifier becomes... That's where you can help!

Your involvement helps the robots help the scientists. Click here to lend your eyes and a few click of your mouse to help us understand and identity more of our undersea world.

This system was developed to track multiple Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) during field ops. It provides real-time updates of the locations of all the platforms relative to the ships and / or work boats that deploy them. It allows for custom or pre-existing map layers to be loaded both online and offline which allows for the easy identification of hazards, provides context and also gives a pretty backdrop to the map.

Given the system is web-based, it is relatively straight forward to extend the system to push updates to shore, provided we have satellite or 3G connectivity while out at sea (which is often not the case).
